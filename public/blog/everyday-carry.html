Everyday Carry

At some point, I started wearing pants that had lots of pockets. It was one of my early attempts at being anywhere near fashionable, and it worked in my eyes. As a side effect from wearing cargos, I was left with four more pockets than your typical pair of pants. I didn't bring much stuff with me in those days, so the pockets stayed mostly empty.

But as anyone who's ever played a video game knows, empty inventory unavoidably fills up over time. You find, collect, or buy cool shit and it goes with you. Eventually, you have so much crap on you that you have to start optimizing your loadout. You stash away the junk and try to maintain a balance of practical things and cool things readily accessible. (Possibly more cool than practical.) You might even get a bag or backpack for the lesser-used stuff. You want to have the means of solving the most problems while carrying the fewest things.

Once you've got your pockets organized, you tend to stick with your setup for a while. It's familliar, personal, and ... fun? These days, four things come with me pretty much everywhere I go: a phone, a knife, lockpicks, and a Flipper.

The phone speaks for itself: taking photos, contacting friends, getting directions, making purchases, etcetera. The world runs on phones and it's beyond inconvenient to not have one. Despite the implications of your every movement being tracked by a giant corporation—which is a topic to unpack at a different time—a phone is probably worth having around, if not just for its emergency uses.

While the phone is same old same old, the blade on my waistband ranges from a three-inch tanto point to a tiny box cutter to nothing at all. Depending on where I'm going, the level of personal risk, and the legality of concealed pointy things, I always (and you as well, hopefully) consider what's reasonable and what's not. Knives aren't just for self-defense either. They can fix a lot of the unexpected or "dang, I wish I had a pair of scissors to trim this" problems that might drive you insane if left unsolved.

On the topic of emergency tools, just about everyone who's seen me with lockpicks has asked why I have them, and I can't say that I have any day-to-day reason. It's ultimately for the edge cases and accidents, and because lockpicks look cool as fuck. Lockpicking can be a life-saving skill, and there's not really a point to knowing how if your tools are back at home when you suddenly need them. I keep them on me, and they do come in handy every so often.

Another item that catches attention is my Flipper Zero. It hangs from a carabiner.
