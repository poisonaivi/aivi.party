world wide webbing

I published my first website on 2024-02-22. Back then, I had no clue what I was doing. I figured out some HTML in school, then followed video tutorials until I deployed my first static site. It was arguably a pretty ugly thing, but I loved the process and the possibilities.


Eight months later, on 2024-10-19, I landed on the design that ultimately became the site you see now. A pure black background, two central columns, and a pixelated appearance. I was so proud of it that I finally decided to buy a custom domain for it, amethystx.net.


An entire year passed before the next major revision, but on 2026-01-06, I bought a new domain, aivi.party, and started working on an experimental recode using CloudFlare Workers and Fetch API. I also decided I would try writing more, and that's what I've been doing since.


My original intent for making this website is a bit unclear. Maybe as a form of escapism, searching for popularity, or reflecting on my existence. At some point in the process, I found the indie web and truly saw the Internet for what it could be. Not news outlets, not social media, not stock markets, not online games, but just the foundational capabilties of the World Wide Web and the huge space it offers to make whatever the hell you want. The information-transporting systems enmeshing our planet provide so many paths to creativity, and there's a global community of people who've been building on it for decades. With all this beauty hidden behind the scenes, I'm just really happy that I went out of my way to look for it.
